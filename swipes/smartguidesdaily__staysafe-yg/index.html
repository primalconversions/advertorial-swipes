You may be surprised to learn that there are actually 5 different types of fire, and each one requires a different method to safely extinguish.

And if you use the wrong type of extinguisher, you could make the problem much worse.

For example, if you’re dealing with an electrical fire, but you use a foam-based fire extinguisher, you’ll actually spark massive flames that can quickly electrocute you — or even explode.

Or, if you’ve had a kitchen mishap and are dealing with a grease fire, and you use a water-based fire extinguisher, you’ll cause the flaming oil to splash and spread like napalm.

Or, if you use a CO2-based extinguisher in a small room — regardless of which fire you’re putting out — you could rapidly suck out all the oxygen and suffocate.

That means if you’re planning to put out a fire yourself, you need to know two things: First, which type of fire it is… and second, which type of extinguisher you have.
